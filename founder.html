Meet the Visionary

Parth Tekale

Founder & Creative Director

A designer at heart and an environmentalist by conviction, Parth founded The Breathing House with a singular vision: to prove that fashion can be both beautiful and kind to our planet.

The Journey

A Story of Purpose

2026

The Breathing House was born from a moment of quiet realization. After a decade working in the fast fashion industry, Parth Tekale witnessed firsthand the toll that mass production takes on both the environment and the artisans who craft our clothes. Standing in a crowded factory in Mumbai, watching piles of synthetic fabric waste being hauled to landfills, he knew something had to change.

Returning to his hometown in Maharashtra, Parth spent months reconnecting with local artisans whose families had practiced sustainable textile traditions for generations. He learned about organic cotton farming, natural dyeing techniques passed down through centuries, and the profound difference that breathable, natural fabrics make for the people who wear them.

The name "The Breathing House" emerged from this journey—a philosophy that clothing should be a sanctuary for the body, allowing it to breathe naturally while honoring the earth that provides the materials. Each garment would tell a story of transparency, from the farmers who grow the cotton to the artisans who craft each piece.

Today, The Breathing House stands as a testament to Parth's belief that modern fashion need not come at the cost of our planet. Every collection is designed with intention, crafted with care, and delivered with a promise: that you can wear your values without compromising on style.

The Philosophy

A Vision for Tomorrow

"I believe we are at a crossroads in fashion. For too long, we've accepted that style and sustainability exist on opposite ends of the spectrum. My mission is to dissolve that boundary completely. When you put on a garment from The Breathing House, you shouldn't have to choose between looking good and doing good—you simply do both."
